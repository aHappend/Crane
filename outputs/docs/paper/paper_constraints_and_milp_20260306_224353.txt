1) Core scheduling representation
- Block hierarchy (Section 4, lines around 545-560): model is recursively partitioned into blocks/sub-blocks.
- State set for one block with N sub-blocks: S_B = {1..2N-1} (lines around 564-579).
- State workload variable s_i (line around 572): number of sub-batches processed in state i.

2) ScT constraints (Section 5.1)
- Definition of ScT (lines around 698-712): cumulative processed sub-batches per state and sub-block.
- Constraint form equivalence (lines around 713-736): Eq.1~Eq.6.
  - integer/non-negative
  - boundary constraints
  - monotonic cumulative progress
  - dependency-safe ordering
  - cumulative update from s_i

3) MeT constraints (Section 5.2)
- Definition of MeT (lines around 828-850): MeT_D and MeT_S are lower bounds of stored sub-batch ranges in DRAM/SRAM.
- Coupling with ScT (lines around 847-853):
  - SRAM range: (MeT_S, ScT]
  - DRAM range: (MeT_D, ScT]
- Construction constraints Eq.7~Eq.12 (lines around 921-942):
  - integer/non-negative
  - monotonic lower bounds over states
  - bounded by ScT
  - dependency-safe availability
  - SRAM/DRAM capacity constraints

4) Recomputation constraints (training, Section 5.3)
- Forward/backward coupled via ScT and MeT.
- Additional forward-end ordering constraint Eq.13 (lines around 985-989).
- Backward-step ScT constraint Eq.14 (lines around 997-1006).

5) How paper explores optimum (Section 5.5)
- Enumerate candidate sub-batch sizes BS_sub (lines around 1230-1232).
- Build ScT with Eq.1~Eq.6, solve with MILP for compute objective (Eq.22, lines around 1235-1245).
- Keep Top-K1 candidates, then build MeT and solve MILP for traffic objective (Eq.23, lines around 1253-1263).
- Keep Top-K2 candidates and continue evaluation.

6) Hierarchical block optimization (Section 6)
- Step-1 Graph Partition: merge strict linear dependency chains into blocks; branch/merge points form higher-level blocks (lines around 1321-1336).
- Step-2 Gradual Partition: iteratively refine lower-level block partition and re-optimize top-level schedule until convergence (lines around 1337-1370).
